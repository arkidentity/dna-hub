# DNA LIFELINE EXERCISE
## Pre-Launch: Building the Foundation Before Phase 1

### Purpose
This exercise helps DNA group members share their life story with vulnerability and transparency before Phase 1 begins. It moves beyond surface-level introductions to create the depth of connection necessary for true discipleship. A group that knows each other's stories enters Phase 1 already bonded — not working toward it.

---

## THE EXERCISE

### Step 1: Draw Your Lifeline
Draw a line and divide it into sections representing the decades of your life (0-10, 11-20, 21-30, etc.). You only need the decades that apply to your age.

### Step 2: Mark Your Moments
Ask the Holy Spirit to bring to mind the **life-shaping moments** in each decade — both highs and lows. These are events that significantly impacted your identity, faith, or trajectory.

**Mark each event as a high or a low:**
- **HIGH** — Positive, joyful, or breakthrough moments
- **LOW** — Painful, difficult, or crisis moments

Give each event a brief label (a few words is enough).

### Step 3: Add the "God Part"
This is where DNA diverges from a typical testimony exercise. For **each event you marked**, write a brief note about **where God was and what He was doing**.

**Questions to consider:**
- Where was God in this moment? (Even if you didn't see Him at the time)
- What was God teaching you through this?
- How did this event shape your understanding of His character?
- If you couldn't see God then, when did you eventually recognize His hand?
- **For the painful events:** How did God redeem this? Or how is He still redeeming it?

**Be ruthlessly honest:**
- If you didn't see God at the time, say so
- If you're still struggling to see His hand in something, say that
- If an event weakened your faith, acknowledge it

### Step 4: Where You Are Today
At the end of your lifeline, write 2-3 sentences describing **where you are right now** in your journey with Jesus. Not where you wish you were. Not a cleaned-up version. The truth.

**Examples of honest "where I am today" statements:**
- "I'm in this DNA group because I know I need to grow, but I'm honestly scared I won't measure up."
- "I've been following Jesus for 20 years but feel like I'm just going through the motions. I want to be re-ignited."
- "I'm new to faith and everything feels overwhelming. I don't even know what I don't know yet."
- "I've led Bible studies before but never made a disciple who made disciples. I want to learn how."

## FACILITATION GUIDELINES

### Timing
- **Week 1 (Pre-Launch):** Leader goes first, modeling vulnerability (30-40 min)
- **Weeks 2-4:** One group member per week (30-40 min each)
- Groups of four or more members may need a fifth week. The discussion questions fold into the final sharing session or the first Phase 1 meeting.

### Leader Goes First
The leader MUST set the tone by:
- Sharing both highs and lows — not just victories
- Being specific about the "God part" in painful events
- Modeling what it looks like to still be in process
- Demonstrating that vulnerability builds trust, not judgment

### Group Response During Sharing
**DO:**
- Listen without interrupting
- Ask clarifying questions about "the God part" if unclear
- Thank the person for their honesty
- Pray for them based on what they shared

**DON'T:**
- Give advice or try to fix their story
- Spiritualize their pain with clichés
- Compare their journey to yours while they're sharing
- Rush them through painful parts

### Why This Matters for DNA
**This exercise accomplishes three DNA-critical goals:**

1. **Builds the relational foundation for multiplication.** You can't multiply what you haven't bonded over. Surface-level community produces surface-level multiplication.

2. **Reveals spiritual readiness and barriers.** The "God part" shows you who's ready to move forward and who needs healing or discipleship in specific areas before multiplying.

3. **Establishes the DNA culture of transparency.** If Pre-Launch is sanitized testimonies, every phase that follows will be shallow. Deep transformation requires deep honesty from the start.

## CONNECTING TO THE DNA FRAMEWORK

**3D Journal Connection:**
After sharing lifelines, encourage group members to journal about:
- HEAD: What did you learn about God through your story?
- HEART: What emotions surfaced as you revisited these moments?
- HANDS: What is God calling you to do based on your story?

**Listening Prayer Circle Connection:**
After someone shares their lifeline, use the Listening Prayer Circle to:
- Ask God for His perspective on their story
- Invite God to speak healing or clarity over painful events
- Listen for how God wants to use their story to help others

**Flow Assessment Connection:**
The lifeline often reveals obstacles to spiritual flow:
- Unhealed wounds blocking discipleship
- Shame preventing vulnerability
- Theological misunderstandings about God's character
- Unresolved bitterness toward God or others

## SAMPLE LEADER SCRIPT

"Before we officially launch into Phase 1, I want us to do something that I think will change how we do everything together.

We're going to spend the next few weeks really getting to know each other. Not the Sunday morning version — the real version. We're going to do an exercise called the DNA Lifeline.

Here's how it works: you map out the decades of your life, mark the moments that shaped you — highs and lows — and then for each one, you write what God was doing. Even if you couldn't see Him at the time.

I'm going first because I want you to see what this looks like. I'm going to share things that are hard for me to talk about. I'm going to tell you where I couldn't see God and where I'm still figuring out what He was doing.

The goal isn't to impress each other with perfect faith stories. The goal is to let each other in so we can actually walk this discipleship journey together.

Jesus built His discipleship method on doing life together — all of life, not just the pretty parts. So that's what we're doing before we do anything else.

I'll go today. Then we'll hear from one person each week until everyone has shared. After that, we launch into Phase 1 — and we'll do it as people who actually know each other.

Sound good? Alright. Here's my story."

## FOLLOW-UP DISCUSSION QUESTIONS

After all lifelines are shared (final Pre-Launch session or first Phase 1 meeting):

1. What patterns did you notice across our stories?
2. Where did you see God showing up consistently, even when people couldn't see Him at the time?
3. What has this exercise revealed about who we are as a group?
4. How does knowing each other's stories change how we'll walk through DNA together?
5. What would it look like for us to help each other see God's hand in the stories that are still being written?

---

*Pre-Launch exercise. Typically 4 weeks (leader + 3 members). Groups of 4+ members may take 5 weeks. Discussion questions fold into the final sharing session or the first Phase 1 meeting.*
