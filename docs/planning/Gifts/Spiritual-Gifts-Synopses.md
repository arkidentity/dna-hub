# Spiritual Gifts Assessment - Synopsis Combinations

**Total Synopses Needed: 202**
- Tier 1: 110 combinations (11 primary × 10 secondary)
- Tier 2: 72 combinations (9 primary × 8 secondary)
- Tier 3: 20 combinations (5 primary × 4 secondary)

---

## Synopsis Writing Guidelines

### Structure (150-250 words each)

**Paragraph 1: Primary Gift Definition**
- Define the primary gift clearly
- Root it in Scripture
- Explain what it looks like in practice

**Paragraph 2: Secondary Gift Integration**
- Show how the secondary gift complements/enhances the primary
- Give practical examples of the combination in action
- Highlight the unique synergy

**Paragraph 3: Practical Application & Next Steps**
- Suggest ways to activate/use this combination
- Offer growth opportunities
- Encourage stewardship of these gifts

### Tone & Style
- Identity-first: "You are beloved FIRST, gifted SECOND"
- Encouraging and affirming, not prescriptive
- Practical and actionable
- Accessible language (avoid overly theological jargon)
- Focus on Kingdom impact and serving others

### Key Principles
1. **No comparison** - Don't suggest this combination is "better" or "worse" than others
2. **Both gifts matter** - Don't minimize the secondary gift
3. **Biblical foundation** - Reference Scripture where appropriate
4. **Practical examples** - Show what this looks like in real life
5. **Call to action** - End with encouragement to step into these gifts

### Serving (Primary)

#### Serving + Teaching
You have a **gift of serving** that is powerfully enhanced by **teaching**. Your primary calling is to meet practical needs and make things happen behind the scenes, but you do it with a unique ability to explain, instruct, and equip others. You don't just serve—you show others *how* to serve effectively.

This combination makes you a practical discipler. You might find yourself naturally training volunteers, creating how-to guides, or mentoring others in hands-on ministry. Your teaching doesn't happen from a stage—it happens in the kitchen, the warehouse, the setup crew, wherever the work is being done. You make complex tasks simple and empower others to contribute with confidence.

**How to activate this combination:** Look for opportunities to serve in areas where you can also train others. Lead a team, create instructional resources, or mentor new volunteers. Your greatest impact comes when you multiply yourself by teaching others to serve with excellence. Don't just do the work—raise up others to do it with you.

#### Serving + Encouraging
You have a **gift of serving** paired with **encouragement**, making you a life-giving presence in every room you enter. You meet practical needs with a smile, a kind word, and genuine care for people's hearts. Your service isn't transactional—it's relational. You make people feel seen, valued, and loved while you're helping them.

This combination makes you the person everyone wants on their team. You set up chairs and tell people they matter. You prepare meals and speak hope into weary hearts. You clean up after events and leave encouraging notes. Your acts of service carry emotional and spiritual weight because they're wrapped in affirmation and kindness.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve intentionally in roles that allow personal connection—hospitality teams, care ministries, support groups. Write encouraging notes while you serve. Speak life into people while you're meeting their needs. Your service becomes a ministry of presence, not just productivity. Let your encouragement flow naturally as you work alongside others.

#### Serving + Giving
You have a **gift of serving** combined with **generosity**, creating a powerful blend of action and provision. You don't just meet needs with your hands—you meet them with your resources. Whether it's time, money, skills, or materials, you're quick to say "I'll take care of that" and follow through with excellence.

This combination makes you a behind-the-scenes hero. You're the one who quietly funds the project, buys the supplies, covers the cost, and then stays to help set it up. You don't seek recognition—you just want to see the Kingdom advance. Your generosity is often expressed through tangible service, making your impact both immediate and lasting.

**How to activate this combination:** Look for ministry opportunities where your resources and labor can work together. Sponsor events and volunteer at them. Fund projects and help execute them. Partner with leaders who have vision but lack resources. Your dual capacity to give and serve makes you uniquely positioned to turn ideas into reality. Don't wait to be asked—step in and meet the need.

#### Serving + Administration
You have a **gift of serving** paired with **administration**, making you the person who doesn't just work hard—you work *smart*. You see what needs to be done, create systems to make it efficient, and execute with precision. You bring order to chaos and turn good intentions into sustainable processes.

This combination makes you invaluable in any ministry context. You're the one who organizes the volunteer schedule, streamlines the workflow, and makes sure nothing falls through the cracks. You don't just show up to serve—you make sure the entire operation runs smoothly. Leaders breathe easier knowing you're on the team because you think ahead and plan well.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve in leadership support roles—event coordination, volunteer management, operations, logistics. Create systems that make serving easier for everyone. Build teams, delegate tasks, and execute flawlessly. Your gift is not just doing the work but organizing the work so it's sustainable and scalable. Lead with structure and serve with excellence.

#### Serving + Mercy
You have a **gift of serving** combined with **mercy**, making you exceptionally compassionate in how you meet practical needs. You don't just fix problems—you care deeply about the people affected by those problems. Your service is gentle, patient, and sensitive to the pain and struggles of others.

This combination makes you a healing presence in people's lives. You might serve in care ministries—meal trains for the sick, support for the grieving, practical help for those in crisis. You don't rush in with solutions—you sit with people in their pain while also meeting their tangible needs. Your presence communicates: "You're not alone, and you matter."

**How to activate this combination:** Serve in ministries that care for the hurting—hospital visitation, grief support, crisis relief, refugee care. Offer practical help wrapped in compassion. Be the one who shows up with a meal and stays to listen. Your service becomes an expression of God's mercy, meeting both physical and emotional needs. Let your heart guide your hands.

#### Serving + Hospitality
You have a **gift of serving** amplified by **hospitality**, making you the ultimate host and helper. You don't just open your home—you create environments where people feel welcome, cared for, and at ease. Whether it's setting up a church event or hosting a small group, you think of every detail to make people feel at home.

This combination makes you a community builder. You're the one who notices when someone's alone and invites them in. You prepare spaces that feel warm and inviting. You anticipate needs before they're voiced. Your service isn't just functional—it's formational, creating spaces where relationships deepen and people encounter God's love through your care.

**How to activate this combination:** Host gatherings intentionally—small groups, dinners, prayer nights, outreach events. Serve on hospitality teams at church. Create welcoming environments wherever you go. Your gift turns ordinary moments into sacred spaces. Don't underestimate the Kingdom impact of a well-set table, a warm greeting, and a listening ear.

#### Serving + Evangelism
You have a **gift of serving** paired with **evangelism**, making your acts of service a powerful witness to the gospel. You don't just meet needs—you meet needs *in Jesus' name* and naturally share why you do what you do. Your service opens doors for conversations about faith, and people see Jesus through your hands and heart.

This combination makes you a missionary in everyday life. You might serve at community events, volunteer in your neighborhood, or meet practical needs for non-believers—and through your service, you build bridges for the gospel. Your evangelism isn't pushy or awkward; it's organic and compelling because it's rooted in tangible love.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve outside the church walls—community service projects, neighborhood cleanups, helping neighbors, volunteering at schools. Let your service create relational equity, then share your story. Be ready to answer the question, "Why are you doing this?" Your hands and your words work together to point people to Jesus.

#### Serving + Worship/Music
You have a **gift of serving** combined with **worship/music**, making you someone who serves *through* creative expression and also serves the worship experience itself. You might serve behind the scenes in worship ministry—sound, lights, stage setup—or you might use your musical gifting to serve other ministries (leading worship for kids, seniors, small groups).

This combination makes you a vital part of creating environments where people encounter God. You understand that worship doesn't just happen on stage—it happens through the preparation, the setup, the execution. Whether you're mixing sound or leading a song, you're serving the worship experience with excellence and humility.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve on worship teams (tech, stage crew, musicians) or use your musical gifts to serve in other contexts (nursing homes, outreach events, community gatherings). See your role as facilitating encounters with God, not performing. Your service creates space for worship, and your worship is an act of service.

#### Serving + Intercessory Prayer
You have a **gift of serving** paired with **intercessory prayer**, making you someone who serves not just with your hands but with your prayers. You might quietly pray over every task you do, intercede for the people you serve, or serve in prayer ministries that support the broader church body.

This combination makes you a spiritual anchor in practical work. You don't just set up the room—you pray over it. You don't just prepare the meal—you pray for those who will eat it. Your service is soaked in prayer, and your prayers are expressed through tangible acts of care.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve with a prayer mindset—pray as you work, pray for those you serve, pray over events before they happen. Join prayer teams that support other ministries (praying for the sick, for missions, for leaders). Your behind-the-scenes intercession fuels Kingdom work in unseen but powerful ways. Let prayer be the foundation of your service.

#### Serving + Craftsmanship/Arts
You have a **gift of serving** combined with **craftsmanship/arts**, making you someone who creates beautiful, functional things for Kingdom purposes. You might build sets for church, design graphics, craft decorations, or create environments that enhance ministry experiences. Your artistry serves the mission.

This combination makes you a culture-shaper in your community. You don't just serve—you elevate the experience through beauty and creativity. You understand that aesthetics matter, that environments affect people, and that well-crafted work honors God and serves others.

**How to activate this combination:** Serve in creative ministries—stage design, graphic design, decoration teams, building projects. Use your artistic skills to enhance ministry environments. Create resources (bulletins, signage, digital content) that serve the mission. Your art is an act of service, and your service is an expression of God's creativity.

### Teaching (Primary)

#### Teaching + Serving
You have a **gift of teaching** strengthened by **serving**, making you a humble, others-focused instructor. You don't teach from a ivory tower—you get in the trenches with people, modeling what you teach and serving those you equip. Your teaching is practical because it's rooted in real-world service.

This combination makes you an accessible, relatable teacher. You might teach a class and then stay after to help clean up. You train volunteers and then serve alongside them. Your students see that you practice what you preach, and that builds trust and respect.

**How to activate this combination:** Teach in contexts where you can also serve—training volunteers, leading workshops, discipling individuals. Don't just deliver content; model it. Serve those you teach by anticipating their needs, creating helpful resources, and walking with them in application. Your teaching gains authority through your servant heart.

#### Teaching + Encouraging
You have a **gift of teaching** paired with **encouragement**, making your instruction life-giving and hope-filled. You don't just inform—you inspire. Your teaching builds people up, affirms their potential, and helps them believe they can grow and change. You see the best in your students and call it out.

This combination makes you a transformational teacher. People leave your classes feeling equipped *and* empowered. You correct with kindness, challenge with grace, and speak truth wrapped in affirmation. Your encouragement makes hard truths easier to receive and difficult concepts more accessible.

**How to activate this combination:** Teach in contexts where people need both clarity and confidence—new believer classes, leadership development, mentoring relationships. Speak truth with love. Celebrate progress. Affirm growth. Your teaching doesn't just transfer knowledge; it builds people up and sends them out with courage.

#### Teaching + Giving
You have a **gift of teaching** combined with **generosity**, making you someone who gives away knowledge, resources, and opportunities freely. You don't hoard insights—you share them lavishly. You might create free resources, teach without charging, or invest in others' learning without expecting anything in return.

This combination makes you a kingdom-minded educator. You understand that teaching is stewardship, and you're generous with what God has entrusted to you. You might fund educational opportunities for others, provide resources to those who can't afford them, or give your time freely to equip and train.

**How to activate this combination:** Create free teaching resources (videos, guides, courses). Teach in underserved contexts (prisons, shelters, marginalized communities). Invest financially in others' education. Your teaching and generosity work together to remove barriers and make discipleship accessible to all.

#### Teaching + Administration
You have a **gift of teaching** paired with **administration**, making you an organized, systematic educator. You don't just teach well—you structure learning experiences that are clear, logical, and easy to follow. You create curricula, build frameworks, and design programs that make complex topics accessible.

This combination makes you an effective curriculum developer and training coordinator. You think big-picture and long-term. You design discipleship pathways, build training programs, and create sustainable learning environments. Your teaching has structure, progression, and intentionality.

**How to activate this combination:** Develop curriculum, lead training programs, coordinate educational initiatives. Design discipleship tracks for your church. Create systematic Bible studies. Your gift is not just teaching content but organizing learning experiences that produce lasting transformation.

#### Teaching + Mercy
You have a **gift of teaching** combined with **mercy**, making you a patient, compassionate instructor. You teach with sensitivity to people's pain, struggles, and learning differences. You don't shame slow learners or dismiss hard questions. You create safe environments where people can be honest about what they don't understand.

This combination makes you a grace-filled teacher. You meet people where they are, walk with them at their pace, and extend patience when they struggle. Your teaching is tender without being soft on truth. You correct gently and restore carefully.

**How to activate this combination:** Teach in contexts where people are hurting or vulnerable—recovery groups, grief support, trauma-informed ministry. Be patient with those who struggle to learn. Teach truth with gentleness. Your mercy makes your teaching accessible to the broken and the weary.

#### Teaching + Hospitality
You have a **gift of teaching** paired with **hospitality**, making your teaching environments warm, welcoming, and relational. You don't just deliver content—you create community. Your classes feel like family gatherings where people learn together, share meals, and build authentic relationships.

This combination makes you a relational teacher. You might host Bible studies in your home, teach over dinner, or create teaching environments where people feel comfortable asking questions and sharing struggles. Your hospitality makes learning feel safe and inviting.

**How to activate this combination:** Host small group studies, teach in home settings, create welcoming teaching environments. Combine teaching with meals or fellowship. Your hospitality lowers defenses and opens hearts, making your teaching more effective. People learn best when they feel at home.

#### Teaching + Evangelism
You have a **gift of teaching** combined with **evangelism**, making you a compelling communicator of the gospel. You don't just teach believers—you reach unbelievers with clarity and conviction. Your teaching naturally points people to Jesus, answers their questions, and invites them into relationship with God.

This combination makes you an apologist and evangelist. You might teach seeker studies, lead Alpha courses, or engage in public conversations about faith. You make the gospel accessible to skeptics and seekers, answering objections with grace and truth.

**How to activate this combination:** Teach in evangelistic contexts—seeker classes, Q&A forums, apologetics courses. Engage with non-believers who have questions. Your teaching builds bridges for the gospel, removing intellectual barriers and inviting people to encounter Jesus personally.

#### Teaching + Worship/Music
You have a **gift of teaching** paired with **worship/music**, making you someone who teaches *through* worship or teaches *about* worship. You might lead worship with teaching interludes, create theological depth in worship songs, or teach others how to lead worship with biblical integrity.

This combination makes you a worship shepherd. You help people understand *why* we worship, not just how. You bring doctrinal depth to musical expression and help others engage God through song with theological clarity.

**How to activate this combination:** Teach worship theology, lead worship with teaching elements, train worship leaders. Write worship songs with strong biblical content. Your teaching enriches worship, and your worship reinforces your teaching.

#### Teaching + Intercessory Prayer
You have a **gift of teaching** combined with **intercessory prayer**, making your teaching ministry deeply rooted in prayer. You don't just prepare lessons—you pray over them. You intercede for your students, asking God to open eyes, soften hearts, and bring transformation. Your teaching is powerful because it's bathed in prayer.

This combination makes you a prayerful teacher. You might teach on prayer, lead prayer workshops, or train intercessors. Your teaching doesn't rely on your own wisdom—it flows from time spent with God, listening and learning before you ever speak.

**How to activate this combination:** Pray before you teach. Teach about prayer. Lead prayer training or prayer meetings. Intercede for those you teach by name. Your prayers prepare the soil for your teaching to take root. Let prayer be the foundation of your instruction.

#### Teaching + Craftsmanship/Arts
You have a **gift of teaching** paired with **craftsmanship/arts**, making you a creative educator. You teach through visuals, metaphors, stories, and hands-on experiences. You might create teaching videos, design infographics, or use art to illustrate biblical truth. Your creativity makes complex truths accessible and memorable.

This combination makes you an innovative teacher. You engage multiple learning styles, using beauty and creativity to reinforce your message. You don't just lecture—you create experiences that stick with people long after the lesson ends.

**How to activate this combination:** Use multimedia in teaching, create visual resources, teach through creative expression. Design engaging curricula that incorporate art, story, and hands-on learning. Your creativity enhances retention and makes learning enjoyable.
