# Way of Life — Full Tool Spec
*DNA Pathway | Phase 1 | Weeks 6–8*

---

## What This Tool Is

The Way of Life is a personal culture document. It is not a checklist, a set of rules, or a spiritual to-do list. It is a declaration. A disciple names, in their own words, how they live because of who they are in Christ.

Every healthy community has a culture. Every family has one. Every business has one. And you, as a temple of the Holy Spirit, have one too. This tool helps you name it.

Disciples build this document during Phase 1 after foundational gospel truths have been covered (Creed Cards, Communion, Baptism, gospel practices). It lives permanently in their "Who I Am" profile and should be revisited and updated every few months as God continues to form them.

---

## Placement on Pathway

- **Phase:** Phase 1 — Foundation
- **Suggested Week:** Week 6–8 (after gospel foundations are established)
- **Repeatable:** Yes. Disciples are prompted to review and update every 90 days.
- **Lives in:** "Who I Am" profile section (visible to leader on dashboard)
- **Leader notification:** Yes. Leader is notified when a disciple completes or updates their Way of Life.

## Tool Flow (Screen by Screen)

### Screen 1 — Introduction

**Header:** Way of Life

**Body copy:**

*"Every family has a culture. Every healthy organization has one too. Culture isn't a rulebook — it's the answer to the question: 'Who are we, and how do people like us live?'*

*You are a temple of the Holy Spirit. That means you and Jesus have a personal culture together. This is your chance to name it.*

*These aren't rules you're trying to keep. They're declarations of who you already are."*

**Button:** Let's Build It

---

### Screen 2 — How This Works (one-time onboarding, shown on first use only)

**Header:** A Few Things Before You Start

Three tips displayed as cards or a brief scroll:

**1. Identity first, behavior second.**
Don't write what you're trying to do. Write what's true because of who you are. "I read Scripture daily" is a goal. "Because I live with Jesus, I hear his voice every day" is a culture statement.

**2. Write what's real, not what sounds good.**
Your leader will see this. Be honest. A statement you actually live is worth more than ten that sound impressive.

**3. Add more over time.**
This document grows with you. Come back every few months. Add what God is building in you. Update what has changed. This is a living record, not a one-time assignment.

**Button:** Got It, Let's Go

---

### Screen 3–9 — The Seven Categories

Each category screen follows the same structure:

- Category name and icon
- One-sentence framing prompt
- Pre-loaded example statements (collapsible — "See examples")
- Input fields for disciple's own statements
- Minimum: 3 statements required to proceed
- Encouraged: 7 or more
- "Add another statement" button after each entry
- Progress indicator at top (Category 1 of 7)

---

#### Category 1: Devotion
*My relationship with God and daily spiritual practices*

**Prompt:** How do you live with God every day?

**Example statements:**
- I spend time in God's Word every morning before the day begins.
- I hear God's voice and I am obedient to what he says.
- I make space for silence because I believe God speaks to those who listen.
- Prayer is not a last resort for me. It is my first response.
- I worship God not just on Sundays but in the ordinary moments of everyday life.

---

#### Category 2: Family
*How I love, lead, and protect the people closest to me*

**Prompt:** What does it look like to honor God in your home?

**Example statements:**
- I provide for my family because that is what people like me do.
- I cover my family in prayer by name, daily.
- I protect my home spiritually and I take that responsibility seriously.
- I give my family my presence, not just my provisions.
- I speak life and blessing over my spouse and my children.
- I am building something in my home that outlasts me.

---

#### Category 3: Community and Fellowship
*How I live in genuine relationship with other believers*

**Prompt:** What does it look like for you to be in real community?

**Example statements:**
- Fellowship is not optional for me. I need the body of Christ and the body needs me.
- I show up for the people in my life even when it is inconvenient.
- I am someone who is easy to be honest with. I don't make people earn the right to speak into my life.
- I pursue accountability because iron sharpens iron and I want to be sharp.
- I make disciples. That is not a program I participate in — it is who I am.
- I invest in people who are earlier in the journey than I am.

---

#### Category 4: Mission and Evangelism
*How I engage with those who don't yet know Jesus*

**Prompt:** What is your posture toward the people around you who are lost?

**Example statements:**
- I carry the names of lost people with me in prayer because they matter to God and to me.
- I share my faith naturally because my story belongs in the world, not just in the church.
- I look for open doors in everyday conversations because I believe God goes before me.
- Evangelism is not a personality type. It is obedience, and I am obedient.
- I live with urgency because I know eternity is real.

---

#### Category 5: Stewardship
*How I manage money, calling, career, and resources*

**Prompt:** How do you honor God with what he has entrusted to you?

**Example statements:**
- I give generously because I trust God as my provider, not my bank account.
- I am building an inheritance for my children's children, not just myself.
- I work with excellence because everything I do is ultimately for an audience of One.
- I live below my means on purpose so I have margin to be generous.
- My career is not separate from my calling. I bring the kingdom into every room I walk into.
- I do not let debt own me. Financial freedom is part of how I steward the temple.

---

#### Category 6: Health
*How I steward my physical body as a temple of the Holy Spirit*

**Prompt:** How do you honor God with your body and your physical wellbeing?

**Example statements:**
- My body is a temple and I treat it like one.
- I rest without guilt because rest is not laziness — it is obedience.
- I make choices about food, sleep, and movement that reflect long-term stewardship, not short-term comfort.
- I pay attention to my emotional health because wounds that go unaddressed become walls.
- I set boundaries because an empty well has nothing to give.

---

#### Category 7: Serving
*How I show up for the church and use my gifts for others*

**Prompt:** How do you contribute to the body of Christ?

**Example statements:**
- I use my gifts to serve, not to be seen.
- I show up consistently because reliability is a form of love.
- I serve in the church because I am the church, not a consumer of it.
- I look for needs I can meet without being asked.
- I take ownership of what God has given me to carry. I don't wait for someone to hand me a task.

## Return Visit Flow (90-Day Prompt)

When a disciple opens the tool after saving, if 90+ days have passed:

**Banner at top:**
*"It's been a while. Anything God has been working in you that belongs here?"*

They can review existing statements, edit any of them, or add new ones. Each update is date-stamped. Leaders see the full history of updates on the dashboard.

## Leader Guide Notes

**When to introduce this tool:**
Week 6–8 of Phase 1, after Creed Cards and gospel-foundation tools have been completed. The disciple should have enough theological grounding to make statements rooted in Scripture, not just personal preference.

**How to facilitate:**
Give disciples time to complete this individually before a meeting. Then in the meeting, have each person share 2–3 statements that felt most significant. Don't rush this. The conversation that happens around these statements often surfaces the most important things a leader needs to know about a disciple.

**What to watch for:**
- Statements that are vague or purely behavioral (may indicate a disciple who has not yet connected identity to behavior)
- Statements that are overly ambitious and disconnected from current reality (gently ask: "Is this true now, or is this where you want to be?")
- Disciples who struggle to write anything (may indicate a deeper identity issue worth addressing in a one-on-one)

**Dashboard visibility:**
The completed Way of Life and all updates are visible on the disciple's profile in the DNA Dashboard. Leaders are notified when a disciple completes or updates this tool.

## Naming Options (still undecided)

| Name | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Way of Life | Simple, historically grounded, self-explanatory | Slightly generic |
| My Culture with Jesus | Connects directly to "people like us" language | Slightly long for a tool name |
| Personal Culture | Direct, fits DNA vocabulary | Could feel corporate |
| The Trellis | Interesting metaphor (structure that supports growth) | Requires explanation |
| People Like Us | Perfect conceptual anchor | Awkward as a tool name |

**Recommendation:** Way of Life. Short, searchable, theologically resonant (early Christians were called followers of "The Way"), and it stands on its own without needing explanation.
