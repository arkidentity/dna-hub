**PBJ = Prayer, Bible, Journal**

It's a simple daily rhythm that takes **20-30 minutes**:

- **P = Prayer** (10 minutes using 4D Prayer)

- **J = Journal** (5-10 minutes using 3D Journal)

That's it. No seminary degree required. No special training needed. Just you, God, a Bible, and a pen.

## üìñ HOW TO DO PBJ

### STEP 1: PRAYER (10 minutes)
Use the **4D Prayer** framework you'll learn in Week 3:

1. **Devotion** (2-3 min) - Worship and thanksgiving
   - "God, You are..."
   - "Thank You for..."

2. **Declaration** (2-3 min) - Speak truth over yourself
   - "I am who You say I am..."
   - Declare Scripture over your life

3. **Dependence** (2-3 min) - Pray for your needs
   - "I need Your help with..."
   - Bring your requests to God

4. **Defense** (2-3 min) - Intercede for others
   - Pray for family, friends, your DNA group
   - Pray for people you're reaching

---

### STEP 2: BIBLE (5-10 minutes)
**Just read Scripture.** That's it.

**How much?**
- Start with 1 chapter
- Or use a reading plan (YouVersion app, Bible in a Year, etc.)
- Or follow what your DNA leader assigns

**What to read?**
- If you're new to the Bible: Start with the Gospel of John
- If you want to grow deeper: Rotate between Gospels, Psalms, Proverbs, and New Testament letters
- Ask your DNA leader for recommendations

**Don't overthink it‚Äîjust read.**

---

### STEP 3: JOURNAL (5-10 minutes)
Use the **3D Journal** method you'll learn in Week 2:

**The 3 D's:**

1. **DISCOVERY** - What does the text say?
   - "I read [passage]"
   - "I observed..."
   - Write down what stood out to you

2. **DECLARATION** - What is God saying to me?
   - "God, what are You saying to me through this?"
   - Listen and write what you hear
   - Even if it feels simple, write it down

3. **DEVOTION** - What will I do in response?
   - "Today, I will..."
   - Make it specific and actionable
   - This is your obedience commitment

**Pro tip:** Keep it simple. Don't write a sermon. A few sentences per D is enough.

## ‚ùì COMMON QUESTIONS

### "What if I miss a day?"
**Grace, not guilt.** Life happens. Just pick it back up the next day. The goal is consistency over time, not perfection.

### "What if I don't 'feel' anything?"
**Do it anyway.** PBJ isn't about feelings‚Äîit's about faithfulness. Some days will feel powerful. Some days will feel dry. Show up either way.

### "What if I can't hear God during the Journal part?"
**Write what the Scripture says.** God speaks through His Word. Even if you don't hear a "lightning bolt moment," write down what the text is teaching you.

### "Can I do PBJ at different times throughout the day?"
**Yes, but...** It's better to do all three together if possible. The rhythm works best as a connected practice. But if splitting it up is the only way, do that.

### "Do I have to do it in this order (P-B-J)?"
**No.** Many people do B-J-P (Bible, Journal, Prayer). Find what works for you. The order doesn't matter‚Äîthe consistency does.
