# DNA KEY MESSAGING

## Core Tagline
**"DNA isn't accidental discipleship - it's loving people with a plan."**

### Why This Works
- Addresses the "organic vs. intentional" tension that many churches struggle with
- Affirms the relational/"loving people" heart while emphasizing the strategic/"with a plan" structure
- Combats the false dichotomy that discipleship must be either spontaneous OR programmatic
- Positions DNA as the synthesis: deeply relational AND systematically reproducible

### Usage Contexts
- **Marketing materials** - "Tired of hoping discipleship happens organically? DNA isn't accidental discipleship - it's loving people with a plan."
- **Church onboarding** - "You already love your people. DNA gives you the plan to multiply that love through generations of disciple-makers."
- **Objection handling** - When pastors say "We don't want a program, we want organic relationships," respond: "That's exactly why DNA works. It's not a program - it's a plan for loving people through intentional relationships."
- **Launch Guide introduction** - Open with this as the thesis statement for why systematic discipleship isn't cold or mechanical

### Related Concepts to Emphasize
1. **Jesus was organically intentional** - He loved people in their messiness AND had a clear plan for their development
2. **Multiplication requires intentionality** - Accidental addition might happen, but multiplication never does
3. **Love without a plan leaves people stuck** - Good intentions without clear pathways = perpetual spiritual childhood
4. **The plan serves the love, not vice versa** - DNA tools exist to deepen relationships, not replace them

### Expansion Messaging

**Short version:**
"DNA isn't accidental discipleship - it's loving people with a plan."

**Medium version:**
"DNA isn't accidental discipleship - it's loving people with a plan. Jesus didn't accidentally make disciple-makers. He loved His twelve deeply AND moved them systematically from followers to world-changers. DNA gives you the same framework."

**Long version:**
"DNA isn't accidental discipleship - it's loving people with a plan. Many churches try to choose between organic relationships and intentional systems. That's a false choice. Jesus modeled both - He did life with His disciples (organic) while moving them through clear developmental stages (intentional). DNA gives you that same 'organically intentional' framework: tools that deepen relationships, phases that create clarity, and a multiplication system that turns disciples into disciple-makers. Love without a plan leaves people stuck. Plans without love feel mechanical. DNA is both."

### Contrast Statements
- **NOT**: "DNA is a discipleship program you run"
- **BUT**: "DNA is a discipleship plan you live"

- **NOT**: "DNA systematizes relationships"
- **BUT**: "DNA gives your relationships direction"

- **NOT**: "DNA replaces organic community"
- **BUT**: "DNA structures organic community for multiplication"

### Source
Developed during Claude conversation analyzing Renew.org's "Becoming a Disciple Maker" materials, specifically their emphasis on intentionality in disciple-making. Adapted to DNA's context where systematic structure serves relational depth rather than replacing it.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**DON'T SAY:**
- "DNA is the best discipleship system" (sounds arrogant)
- "DNA guarantees multiplication" (overpromises)
- "DNA is simple" (undersells the depth)
- "DNA is just a tool" (undersells the philosophy)

**DO SAY:**
- "DNA is a proven plan for multiplication" (confident, factual)
- "DNA creates the conditions for multiplication" (realistic)
- "DNA is comprehensive but accessible" (acknowledges depth)
- "DNA is a complete system with philosophy and tools" (accurate scope)
